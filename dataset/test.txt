A Call Option in stock market is basically a contract to buy a specific stock, at a specific price on a specific date. When you purchase the stock your risk is unlimited in a sense that you might lose your entire capital or incalculable part of it, but when you purchase calls your risk is only limited to the premium paid. You are under no obligation to purchase the stock. Generally calls of a particular stock are purchased by a person when they are bullish on it or if they wanted to hedge a specific position. Options are riskier and more volatile than the underlying stock. A mere few percent move in a stock may turn out to be over a 100% or more move in the options.
